Climate Change and Its Impact

From rising seas to displaced communities — how a warming planet is reshaping life as we know it.

20267 min readThe Digital Green Journal

Climate change is no longer a warning about the future — it is the defining reality of the present. From record-breaking heatwaves to catastrophic floods, rising seas, and disappearing glaciers, the consequences of a warming planet are unfolding at an accelerating pace across every corner of the Earth.

What is Climate Change?

Climate change refers to long-term shifts in global temperatures and weather patterns. While natural variations have always occurred, the term now predominantly refers to the human-induced changes that have accelerated dramatically since the mid-20th century. The burning of fossil fuels, deforestation, and industrial activity have loaded the atmosphere with greenhouse gases, triggering a cascade of consequences worldwide.

Environmental Impacts

Rising Sea Levels

As global temperatures rise, glaciers and ice sheets melt, adding water to the oceans. Simultaneously, warmer water expands in volume. Sea levels have risen approximately 20 cm since 1900, with the rate accelerating in recent decades. Low-lying coastal nations and island communities face existential threats.

Extreme Weather Events

Climate change makes extreme weather more frequent and intense. Typhoons draw energy from warmer ocean surfaces. Droughts intensify as evaporation increases. Heavy rainfall becomes more extreme as the atmosphere holds more moisture. In the Philippines, the link between warming and increasingly destructive typhoons is well documented.

Wildfires and Heatwaves

Prolonged droughts and elevated temperatures create conditions ripe for wildfires. Countries like Australia, Canada, and the United States have experienced record-breaking fire seasons. Heatwaves are becoming longer, more frequent, and deadlier, with serious consequences for human health, agriculture, and ecosystems.

Social and Human Impacts

🏠

Displacement & Migration

An estimated 216 million people could be displaced within their own countries by 2050 due to climate impacts (World Bank, 2021).

🌾

Food Security

Climate change threatens crop yields. Heat stress, altered rainfall, and droughts put food production at risk, especially in tropical nations.

💧

Water Scarcity

By 2050, up to 5.7 billion people could face water scarcity for at least one month per year due to changing precipitation patterns.

🏥

Public Health

Climate change expands the range of disease vectors, worsens air quality, and increases heat-related illness — a global public health emergency.

⚖️

Inequality & Justice

Communities least responsible for climate change are often the most vulnerable. Climate change deepens existing inequalities worldwide.

💸

Economic Losses

Extreme weather causes trillions in damages globally. The Philippines loses an estimated 0.8–1% of GDP annually to climate-related disasters.

"Climate change is not just an environmental issue. It is a threat multiplier that worsens poverty, hunger, health, displacement, and conflict."
United Nations Development Programme (UNDP)

Impact on the Philippines

The Philippines is consistently ranked among the most climate-vulnerable countries in the world. An archipelago of over 7,000 islands, it faces rising sea levels, more intense typhoons (an average of 20 per year), coastal erosion, coral bleaching, and changing agricultural conditions threatening millions of farmers and fisherfolk.

Typhoon Haiyan (Yolanda) in 2013 — one of the most powerful storms ever recorded at landfall — killed over 6,000 people and displaced more than 4 million. Scientists have linked the intensification of such storms to rising sea surface temperatures caused by climate change.

Philippines Climate Vulnerability — Key Facts

  • Ranked 3rd most affected country by extreme weather in the Global Climate Risk Index
  • Faces an average of 20 typhoons per year, 7–8 of which make direct landfall
  • Sea levels around the Philippines rising at twice the global average rate
  • Coral reefs — vital to fisheries and coastal protection — face widespread bleaching
  • Over 60% of the population lives in coastal areas at risk of flooding and storm surge
